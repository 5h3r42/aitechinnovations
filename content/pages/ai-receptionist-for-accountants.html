The day-to-day problem

Good prospects should not have to wait for a first response.

Accountancy enquiries often start with a straightforward website question. When that answer is hard to find or arrives too late, a potential client can leave before the team has a chance to help.

After-hours gaps

New-client questions arrive when the office is closed, and the visitor may not return the next day.

Repeated admin

Staff repeatedly explain services, onboarding, documents, office hours and the right contact route.

Questions that need care

Client-specific, sensitive and complex enquiries need a clear handover—not a rushed automated answer.

The outcome

A controlled route from website question to staff follow-up.

The receptionist handles the useful first layer, while your practice keeps control of the answers, escalation rules and commercial judgement.

Safeguards and handover

Useful, but always within your firm’s boundaries.

The receptionist is designed to make the first interaction clearer—not to replace professional judgement or staff control.

Approved answers: responses are based on firm-approved services, FAQs and contact information.

No personalised advice: it does not give tax, legal, financial or regulated advice for a visitor’s circumstances.

No invented promises: unsupported pricing, availability and service commitments are not made up.

Human review: sensitive and complex enquiries are escalated, while booking requests remain subject to staff review unless a confirmed calendar integration is agreed.

Staff visibility: conversations and qualified enquiries are captured securely in the CRM for follow-up.

FAQ

Questions about an AI receptionist for accountants.

Does it give tax advice?

No. It is configured to answer approved firm information and hand client-specific, tax, legal, financial or otherwise sensitive questions to staff.

Can it use our existing website?

Yes. The pilot is designed for installation on your existing website, using your agreed services, FAQs and contact routes.

Can staff take over a conversation?

Yes. Sensitive, complex and unsupported enquiries can be routed for staff review and follow-up.

What happens to captured enquiries?

Relevant contact details and enquiry context are routed into AI-Platform CRM so the team can review and follow up.

Can we control the answers?

Yes. The receptionist is configured from approved firm information, with agreed boundaries and handover rules.

Can it work outside office hours?

Yes. It can provide the agreed first response and capture enquiries at any time, while staff follow up during their normal process.

Does it replace staff?

No. It handles the useful first layer and brings the right enquiries to staff with better context.

How long does setup take?

Timing depends on the quality of the available service information, FAQ approval, website access and test feedback. This is confirmed during scoping.

Is there a pilot?

Yes. The introductory offer is a focused 30-day pilot with setup, testing and launch support.

Can we customise the branding?

Yes. The website experience can be configured to fit your firm’s approved branding and contact routes.
