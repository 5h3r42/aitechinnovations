# Objection Handling

Use curiosity and clarity. Do not pressure a prospect or make unsupported claims.

## "It is too expensive"

"I understand. Is the concern the overall investment, the timing, or whether the scope will solve the right problem? We can look at the highest-priority first step and decide whether a smaller scope is more appropriate."

## "We already have a website"

"That makes sense. The useful question is whether it is clearly helping the business generate the enquiries or bookings you need. Would it help if I shared the specific observations behind the recommendation?"

## "We need to think about it"

"Of course. What would be most useful for you to consider, and when should we reconnect after you have had that discussion?"

## "We are too busy"

"Understood. We can define the client inputs up front and keep decisions focused. Is there a quieter date or a smaller first phase that would be more realistic?"

## "Can you guarantee results?"

"We can commit to a clear scope, good practice, transparent communication, and measurement. Results depend on factors beyond a website or campaign, so we would not make guarantees we cannot substantiate."
